About Me

The Story of The Underground Chef of Clarksville Tn Food Truck

Hi there! I’m Ailene Jordan a.k.a. LumpiaQueen, the chef behind The Underground Chef, and I’d love to share my story with you. It all began at home, where I started cooking up delicious meals and selling a couple of plates to provide not just for my family but also for those in need—both here in Clarksville and back home in the Philippines. Cooking has always been my passion, and I wanted to create something special that would bring joy to others.

Then, I was blessed with the opportunity to launch my very own food truck! This was a dream come true. Using only the freshest ingredients, I poured my love and care into every dish I prepared. As I took my food truck out into the streets, I quickly realized it was more than just a place to serve food; it became a gathering spot for friends, families, and food lovers. There’s something magical about sharing a meal together, and I wanted everyone to feel at home when they visited my truck.

As my journey continued, I was honored to receive appreciation and recognition awards, including Best Home-Based Business, Best Chef, Best Cook, and Best Community Leader. These accolades inspire me to keep pushing forward and remind me of the impact we can have when we come together.

But my mission goes beyond just serving tasty meals. With a deep connection to my roots in the Philippines, I felt a strong calling to give back. Every meal sold helps support those in need—both in our local community and back home. It’s my way of spreading kindness and compassion, creating a bridge that connects us all.

I also love celebrating Filipino culture through my cooking. Each dish tells a story, introducing people to unique flavors and traditions that I hold dear. So when you stop by The Underground Chef of Clarksville Tn food truck, know that you’re not just enjoying a great meal—you’re joining me on a journey of connection, compassion, and community. Together, we can savor delicious food while making a difference in the lives of others, one plate at a time.
